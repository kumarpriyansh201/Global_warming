EFFECTS OF GLOBAL WARMING

Graph 1: Temperature

Temperature has increased by 1.1 degrees Celsius since pre-industrial times.

Graph 2: Sea Level

Sea level has risen by 8 inches since 1880.

Graph 3: Carbon Dioxide Levels

Carbon dioxide levels have increased by 45% since pre-industrial times.

Some of the major effects of global warming include:

Rising Sea Levels: As temperatures continue to rise, glaciers and ice caps are melting, causing sea levels to rise. This could lead to flooding in low-lying areas and the displacement of people living in coastal regions.

Extreme Weather: Global warming is causing more frequent and severe weather events, such as hurricanes, tornadoes, heat waves, and droughts. These weather events can have devastating effects on communities, economies, and ecosystems.

Melting Arctic Ice: The Arctic is warming twice as fast as the rest of the world, causing significant melting of sea ice. This is not only affecting wildlife, such as polar bears and walruses, but also the people who rely on these ecosystems for their livelihoods.

Ocean Acidification: The absorption of excess carbon dioxide from the atmosphere by the oceans is causing them to become more acidic. This is having a negative impact on marine life and the entire ocean ecosystem.

Changes in Ecosystems: As temperatures rise, many ecosystems are changing rapidly, affecting plant and animal species that are unable to adapt quickly enough. This can lead to the extinction of species and changes in entire ecosystems.

Public Health Impacts: Global warming is also having a significant impact on public health. As temperatures rise, the spread of diseases such as malaria, dengue fever, and Lyme disease is increasing, and air pollution is causing respiratory problems in many parts of the world.
